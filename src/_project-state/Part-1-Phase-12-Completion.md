## What was done

`/about/` and `/contact/` are now live in both EN and ES, building on the Phase 1.11 design handover end-to-end. About ships six sections (Hero → Brand story → Team → Credentials → Projects teaser → CTA), Contact ships five (Hero → Info+Form → Map → Calendly placeholder → Service-area strip). The locked Phase 1.05 chrome wraps both unchanged. About's projects teaser literally reuses `<HomeProjects>` per handover §3.6 — no new component, no separate curation. Contact's `<ContactForm>` is the only client component on the page; the rest of Contact is server-rendered.

Schema is wired and emits clean: About emits `Person` × 3 (Erick, Nick, Marcin) + `BreadcrumbList`; Contact emits `ContactPage` (mainEntity → sitewide LocalBusiness `@id`) + `BreadcrumbList`. Two new schema helpers landed (`src/lib/schema/person.ts`, `src/lib/schema/contactPage.ts`) so future phases reuse the builders. Two new tiny UI primitives (`<TeamCard>`, `<CredentialBadge>`) compose existing Phase 1.03 tokens — no new component variants. Five gradient + per-pixel-noise placeholder JPGs (hero + brand-story + 3 team cards) generated by a new `scripts/gen-about-placeholders.mjs` matching the Phase 1.07/1.09 pattern.

D11 (no Contact body amber) and D7 (no Contact hero photo) hold: the only amber on `/contact/` is the chrome navbar amber, and the H1 is the LCP candidate. The static map placeholder (D8) and tel: Calendly fallback (D9) keep third-party network load at zero. About's hero (50vh / 40vh) is the LCP candidate with priority + AVIF blur placeholder + explicit dimensions; the placeholder JPG is 300KB (under the 350KB budget). One small deviation from the handover §3.1 spec: About hero now includes a visible `<Breadcrumb variant="on-dark">` above the H1 to satisfy master-plan §2.9's rule that the visible breadcrumb and BreadcrumbList JSON-LD must consume the same items — the audience-landing pattern from Phase 1.08 carried over verbatim. Surface alternation, amber discipline (one body amber on About in `<AboutCTA>`, zero on Contact), and motion choreography (single section-level `<AnimateIn>` on every section, stagger only on the team grid, no per-form-field animation) are all correct.
